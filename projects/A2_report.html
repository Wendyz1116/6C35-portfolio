Subtheme: Zoning and Housing Supply

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. Do areas with a higher percentage of single-family homes tend to have more older or retired people?

    2. Motivation: Growing up in Florida, I always heard people say that it’s a good place to retire to since there is usually more space and larger homes for families. This motivated me to look into whether this is common in Massachusetts, too where people other than 65 tend to live in areas with a higher percentage of single-family home zoning

  2. Do people of color or people with low incomes live in areas with a lower percentage of single-family homes?

    3. In “Houses are too expensive. Apartments are too small. Is this a fix?” (Washington Post) they said that “zoning laws [were] an implicit strategy to price people of color and low-income people out of certain neighborhoods.” This makes me wonder if neighborhoods with a higher percentage of single-family housing end up having fewer people of color and people with lower incomes.

  3. Do areas with a lower percentage of single-family homes have a higher population?

    4. In “Houses are too expensive. Apartments are too small. Is this a fix?” (Washington Post), they claim that with fewer single-family-only zones, there can be more housing options for people. This makes me wonder if there is a correlation between the percentage of single-family housing and the number of people living there.
